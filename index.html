How it works

Keep same sargam pattern keeps the notation exactly as scale degrees. Example: S R G M stays S R G M when changing from C flute to D flute, because you are still thinking relative to the new Sa.

Shift notation to new Hindustani notes is useful when you want the written swaras themselves to change. Example: moving from C to B shifts each written swara down by one semitone, so S R G M becomes N S r g.

Punctuation, spaces, bar lines, commas, dashes, and line breaks are preserved as much as possible.
